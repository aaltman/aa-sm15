Insofar as predictive performance is concerned, the curse of dimensionality is used interchangeably with the peaking phenomenon,[5] which is also known as Hughes phenomenon.[6] This phenomenon states that with a fixed number of training samples, the average (expected) predictive power of a classifier or regressor first increases as the number of dimensions or features used is increased but beyond a certain dimensionality it starts deteriorating instead of improving steadily.[7][8][9]
