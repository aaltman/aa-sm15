Honestly being a manager of managers is easier than being a manager of ICs.

The level of responsibility is much much greater and the repercussions to fucking up are immense, but if you're promoting the right people into leadership positions then your life becomes a lot easier. Why? Because your direct reports have already proven themselves. You can set direction and watch them succeed with generally only minor course corrections.

That said, it's different if you're joining a new company and you're inheriting an existing leadership team that you didn't build yourself. That's why you often see big re-orgs after a new senior leader comes into an organisation.

Managing ICs is a lot harder because you generally have varying levels of skill or experience. One developer can easily take up 80% of your time. That's generally a sign that it's not working out and then you have to exercise the shittiest part of your job.
