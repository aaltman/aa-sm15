: What's on the horizonI happen to have insider knowledge that it is possible to make a wait-free universal construct that is fast enough to be used in production, because it doesn't have to copy the entire data structure (except on very rare occasions). This new way of doing WFUCs is fast enough to be used in real-life applications, in fact, it's capable of beating hand-written lock-free and wait-free data structures.
Yes, it's that good. We call it CX and the paper is almost ready.
... did I say it does wait-free memory reclamation as well?  ;)

As soon as other researchers see what CX is capable of, I'm sure they will have their own ideas and improve upon it, thus bringing about a new age of production-capable WFUCs.
