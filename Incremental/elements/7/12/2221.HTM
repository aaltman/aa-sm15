Two possibilities:

  1. Multiple leaders, transactions replicated synchronously

Each leader can accept writes but cannot unilaterally commit a transaction. There must be consensus among the leaders before a transaction is committed, using something like a two phase commit algorithm. This means that you can have multiple leaders accepting write transactions, but transactions aren't committed until they are accepted by a quorum of leaders.

2) Multiple leaders, transactions replicated asynchronously

Leaders can accept writes and commit transactions independently from one another. The committed transactions are replicated to other nodes, but you must account for the possibility that two transactions accepted by different leader nodes are inconsistent with each other. It's usually up to the application to determine how such inconsistencies are resolved.

The second approach provides better scaling, but breaks one or other of the ACID guarantees - consistency or durability, for example.
