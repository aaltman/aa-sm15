: What does the HTTP Observatory test?

The HTTP Observatory tests the following security features:

  • Secure cookie configuration: Access to cookies should be locked down as much as possible, to prevent attacks such as Cross-site scripting (XSS) and Cross-site request forgery (CSRF).
  • Cross-origin Resource Sharing (CORS): Limit cross-origin resource access to no more than necessary.
  • Content Security Policy (CSP): Provides fine-grained control over the locations from which resources on a site can be loaded.
  • Strict-Transport-Security: Enforces site connections via HTTPS only, to prevent manipulator-in-the-middle (MiTM) attacks.
  • Correct redirection behavior to HTTPS on initial loading: Prevents attacks during initial loading.
  • Referrer-Policy: Prevents leaking of sensitive information.
  • Subresource Integrity (SRI): Verify that fetched resources are delivered without unexpected manipulation.
  • X-Content-Type-Options: Enforce correct resource MIME types, to further mitigate XSS attacks.
  • Content-Security-Policy: frame-ancestors and X-Frame-Options: Limit the ability of other sites to embed your site in an <iframe>, helping to prevent clickjacking.
  • Cross-Origin-Resource-Policy: Prevent certain requests from other origins accessing resources on your site, to mitigate speculative side-channel attacks like Spectre.
