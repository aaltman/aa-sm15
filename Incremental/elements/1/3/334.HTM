: Psychogenic fugue[edit]
Main article: Fugue state

Psychogenic fugue, a form of psychogenic amnesia, is a DSM-IV Dissociative Disorder in which people forget their personal history, including who they are, for a period of hours to days following a trauma.[42] A history of depression as well as stress, anxiety or head injury could lead to fugue states.[43] When the person recovers they are able to remember their personal history, but they have amnesia for the events that took place during the fugue state.
