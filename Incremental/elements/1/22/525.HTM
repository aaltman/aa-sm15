: Economic development until mid-1948[edit]

Prior to 1948, the Yugoslav economy relied on state-controlled trade of agricultural products and raw materials to the USSR in exchange for processed goods and machinery.[45] Overall shortages of machinery and local shortages of labour—especially skilled experts—plagued the economy.[46] As the conflict with Stalin progressed, Yugoslavia decided to become self-sufficient and develop its military capabilities, which led to greater budget expenditures for infrastructure and personnel,[47] and the establishment of research and development institutions. To offset the lack of machinery, third shifts were introduced in factories.[48] Authorities mobilised, under threat of imprisonment, those not otherwise employed, and peasants not actively doing agricultural work, to work in mines extracting coal or ores for export, or at construction sites.[49] Food and fuel were stockpiled for the military,[50] leading to market shortages.[51]
