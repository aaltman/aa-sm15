: The tools

All sane individuals know how to solve problems. The greatest problem solvers have an excellent toolset without ever formalizing it. However, a simple formal model of problem solving translated into a formula expressed in simple language can make a world of a difference for everyone. In my formula below, I introduce a couple of concepts that many problem solvers sense intuitively, but do not employ extensively or systematically as these concepts are not defined clearly enough in their mind. Here are the most important problem solving tools I would like you to carry away from this text:

  • mental computation, i.e. simple language explanation of how the brain goes about solving problems (no anatomy or neural network knowledge needed)
  • protected mental computation, i.e. how to give the brain perfect peace time for thinking
  • disruptive learning, i.e. how to disrupt the brain's peace for the sake of a creative breakthrough
  • brainstorming and incremental brainstorming, i.e. how to employ other people's brains in the job
  • creative forgetting, i.e. how to forget old wrong solution pathways to find the right one
  • natural creativity cycle, i.e. how to solve problems in sleep without lifting a finger
